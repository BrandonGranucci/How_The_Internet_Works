IP Addresses, Packets, and Routing

  • Every computer has it's own address, known as an Internet Protocol address
  • The old IP addresses is known as the IPV4 which allows for IP addresses that contain up to 16 different numbers
  • Currently the world is transitioning to IPV6, a potential combination of up to 32 numbers and/or letters
  • IPV6 will change the number of potential addresses from 4 billion to 340 trillion
  • Data transfers over the net are broken into packets and sent to many different checkpoints before reaching their destination
  • Every machine has it's own IP address they use to know where to send packets to
